Digital Ballot Previews Let Voters See Exactly What's on Their Ballot Before Election Day

By James Thornton · March 6, 2026 · 5 min read

For decades, voters have walked into polling places unsure of what they'd find on their ballot — especially in off-year elections packed with local measures, judicial retentions, and obscure board seats. A new digital ballot preview tool is changing that by giving every voter a personalized look at their exact ballot before they ever leave home.

The concept is simple but powerful: enter your home address, and the platform returns a complete preview of every race, ballot measure, and referendum you'll be asked to vote on. Each item includes plain-language explanations, candidate information, and links to deeper research.

"No more surprises in the voting booth," said election accessibility advocate Diana Reyes. "When people know what's coming, they vote with more confidence and they're less likely to skip races they don't recognize."

Addressing the Down-Ballot Drop-Off

Studies have consistently shown that voter participation drops dramatically for races further down the ballot. In many jurisdictions, 20-30% of voters who cast a ballot for president leave local races completely blank. Ballot preview tools directly address this problem by giving voters time to research every contest in advance.

County election officials in several states have already expressed interest in linking to the tool from their official voter information pages, calling it a natural complement to existing outreach efforts.

"Anything that reduces confusion and increases preparedness is a win for election administration," said county clerk Robert Halverson. "We spend millions on voter education — this kind of technology multiplies that investment."

The platform is free for voters to use and is available in both English and Spanish, with additional language support planned for later this year.
