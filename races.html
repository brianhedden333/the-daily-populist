Interactive Race Tracker Maps Every Contest From City Council to Congress

By Emily Foster · March 6, 2026 · 5 min read

Election coverage has traditionally focused on a handful of high-profile races — the presidency, a few competitive Senate seats, and the occasional governor's race. But thousands of consequential elections take place every cycle at the state and local level, often with little public attention. A new interactive race tracker is aiming to change that by mapping every contest in the country and making detailed race information available to every voter.

The platform provides a comprehensive, searchable directory of races at every level of government. Users can explore contests by location, office type, or competitiveness, and drill down into individual races to see candidate profiles, polling data, fundraising totals, and key dates.

"We built this because we believe every race matters," said the platform's lead designer. "A school board election in a small town can shape a generation of students. A county commissioner race can determine whether a new hospital gets built. These races deserve attention."

Interactive Maps and District Overlays

One of the most popular features is an interactive map that lets users visualize races geographically. Color-coded overlays show district boundaries, partisan lean, and electoral competitiveness, giving users an at-a-glance view of the political landscape in their area.

Journalists and newsrooms have also embraced the tool as a research resource, using it to identify under-covered races and build data-driven election coverage.

"It's become an indispensable part of our election planning," said metro editor Sandra Weeks. "We can quickly identify the most competitive local races and allocate our reporting resources accordingly."

The tracker is updated continuously as new candidates file, polls are released, and results come in on election night, providing a living map of American democracy in action.
